Innovative Research Space

“Where Curiosity Creates Change”

At the University of Denver, we drive discovery, inspire innovation, and empower students and faculty to create real-world impact through bold, collaborative research as an R1 institution.

Computational Biomechanics

Computational models uses to evaluate orthopaedic implants, improving design and patient outcomes. The lab focuses on joint mechanics and musculoskeletal systems to understand how implants affect human motion, enhancing healthcare and treatments for musculoskeletal issues.

Probabilistic Mechanics

Studies how variability in mechanical systems affects performance, using probabilistic analysis and Monte Carlo simulations. This research improves understanding of joint mechanics and materials properties, optimizing designs in orthopaedics and nanotechnology for safer, more reliable solutions.

Orthopaedic Biomechanics

Applies engineering principles to study joint mechanics, human motion, and implant testing. Using experimental and computational methods, it develops innovative solutions to improve musculoskeletal health and enhance implant design, advancing medical technologies for better patient outcomes.
