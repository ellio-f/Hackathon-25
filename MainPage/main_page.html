Computational Biomechanics

Computational models uses to evaluate orthopaedic implants, improving design and patient outcomes. The lab focuses on joint mechanics and musculoskeletal systems to understand how implants affect human motion, enhancing healthcare and treatments for musculoskeletal issues.

Probabilistic Mechanics

Studies how variability in mechanical systems affects performance, using probabilistic analysis and Monte Carlo simulations. This research improves understanding of joint mechanics and materials properties, optimizing designs in orthopaedics and nanotechnology for safer, more reliable solutions.

Orthopaedic Biomechanics

Applies engineering principles to study joint mechanics, human motion, and implant testing. Using experimental and computational methods, it develops innovative solutions to improve musculoskeletal health and enhance implant design, advancing medical technologies for better patient outcomes.

Engineering Innovation: Robots Providing Social Support

Writer: Kevin Douglas (BA 2020)

Dream Face Technologies, founded by Dr. Mohammad H. Mahoor and led alongside principal engineer Josh Lane, focuses on developing social robots to support individuals with autism, depression, Alzheimer’s, and dementia. Their social robot, Ryan, is designed to improve users’ quality of life by providing companionship, medication reminders, conversation, mood recognition, and cognitive stimulation through games and reminiscence activities. Beginning with early prototypes like the "eBear," the team has advanced Ryan through multiple iterations, incorporating extensive research and user feedback to create a versatile, supportive tool for senior care environments.

The University of Denver has provided significant support to Dream Face Technologies through access to engineering facilities, resources, and a strong pool of student and alumni talent.The university’s commitment to fostering innovation and entrepreneurship has played a critical role in the company's growth and success. Dream Face Technologies remains closely connected to the DU community, with approximately 80% of its team composed of DU students and graduates.
